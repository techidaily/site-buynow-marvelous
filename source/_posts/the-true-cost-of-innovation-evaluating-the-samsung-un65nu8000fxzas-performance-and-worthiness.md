## Setup: Nice and simple

 If you‚Äôve ever set up a cable modem, then you‚Äôll breeze through the setup on the C3700\. We just unplugged our existing cable modem, plugged all the cables into this new one, and waited for the lights to turn on. Then all we had to do was phone our ISP to activate it.

## Connectivity: Everything a small home needs

 The Netgear C3700 is a modem-router combo, which means, hypothetically, it‚Äôs the only networking device many people will need. On top of the two Gigabit[Ethernet](https://www.lifewire.com/what-is-an-ethernet-cable-817548) ports, you‚Äôre also getting dual antennas, which makes this a dual-band device (2.4GHz and 5.0GHz). It‚Äôs not the fastest wireless connection in the world, though‚Äîthe Netgear C3700 is rated for N600 speeds, something we haven‚Äôt seen in a few years.

 It is an 8x4 DOCSIS 3.0 modem, meaning it‚Äôs capable of handling internet speeds up to 340 Mbps. But that N600 wireless speed means that devices on Wi-Fi won‚Äôt be that fast. If you have a really fast internet connection like we do, it might be best to use an external wireless router.

 There is also a USB 2.0 port around the back where you can plug in a hard drive or a printer for network access.

## Network Performance: Great when wired, but wireless speeds lag

 We didn‚Äôt exactly have high hopes for the C3700 and its N600 wireless rating. At its peak during testing, we reached wireless speeds of 130Mbps, but that didn‚Äôt last‚Äîwith multiple devices connected and in use, we were seeing speeds as low as 40Mbps just a few feet away from the router.

 We weren‚Äôt impressed with the wireless performance, but the wired performance surprised us. Even after connecting 13 devices to our network, we were able to get our advertised 250 Mbps speeds through a wired connection, and sometimes even higher.

 Thankfully, this means that an attached router would be able to keep up, which is what we‚Äôd recommend. The wireless performance isn‚Äôt great and you‚Äôre going to have a much better experience with a separate router.

## Software: No frills

 We prefer a network backend that‚Äôs straight to the point, and that‚Äôs exactly what we got with the Netgear C3700.

 The management portal is incredibly simple. Upon logging in, there are six tiles that you can click on to change settings like your wireless password, attached devices, and anything connected to the USB port. These tiles will also show you the status of your network at a glance: how many devices are attached, whether your cable connection is working, and even your network password.

 We were able to click over into the ‚Äúadvanced‚Äù tab to gain access to more robust controls, but this shouldn‚Äôt be necessary for most users. Power users will probably appreciate the fine controls.

 You can also download the Netgear Genie app on your phone, which allows you to manage your network from a more attractive interface. Much of the same functionality is here, but it might be a more approachable interface for those who don‚Äôt really know where to start with their router settings.

## Price: It pays for itself

 The Netgear C3700 retails for $109.99, but you should be able to find it for less (as of the time of this writing, it‚Äôs on sale for about $95). At this price, the modem has a head start on paying for itself.

 For an 8x4 DOCSIS 3.0 cable modem with a lightweight router built in, this price is about par for the course. If you can find the Netgear C3700 for under $100, then it might be worth buying for the cable modem performance and pairing it with a solid wireless router.

However, the Arris model does not have a USB 2.0 port for network-attached storage or printers.

 Final Verdict

 **A solid modem, but the poor wireless performance undermines its appeal as a combo device.**
 The cable modem‚Äôs performance is great, but the wireless performance is just not there. If all you want is the cheapest possible modem with built-in dual-band Wi-Fi, then the Netgear C3700 checks those boxes. But we think you‚Äôre better off spending more for better performance or just buying two standalone devices.
