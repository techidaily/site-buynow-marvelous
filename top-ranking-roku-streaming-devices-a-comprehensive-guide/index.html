What to Look For in a Roku TV in 2023

Apart from the platform built into the television set itself, Roku smart TVs aren’t that different from other televisions on the market. If you’re looking for an excellent Roku TV, you want the television to be great in general and come with Roku built-in.

Picture quality is one of the most important things to consider with any television. You want the television to offer a sharp and vibrant image with an excellent contrast ratio. As there are no OLED Roku TVs on the market, features like quantum dot , full-array local dimming , and Mini-LED have become very important. These technologies help LED TVs improve their picture performance multi-fold.

If you are buying a Roku TV for gaming, there are additional features that you need to look for, like low input lag, fast response time, HDMI 2.1 support, and variable refresh rate (VRR). These ensure you get a fantastic gaming experience and that your TV is future-proof.

Budget and TV size also play a significant role in your purchase decision. As such, our recommendations include the best Roku TVs for a variety of budgets. For screen size, we have a handy guide to help you figure out the right TV size for your room.

For a deeper dive into which TV features are most important, we have a great explainer on everything you need to know before buying a TV . Now, let’s jump into our recommendations.

Best Roku TV Overall: Roku Plus Series

Roku

Pros Cons
Excellent picture quality No advanced gaming features
Dolby Vision and HDR10+ support

As TCL hasn’t introduced any new mid-range or high-end Roku TV models in 2023 and doesn’t seem to be restocking the older models, the Roku TV selection is pretty slim. So, if you want the best Roku TV in 2023, Roku’s own Plus Series is your best option. It’s an excellent 4K mid-range television that delivers a great picture overall, thanks to the assistance of quantum dots and full-array local dimming.

The TV also shines in terms of its coverage of the DCI-P3 color space at over 95%. So you’ll get accurate color in HDR. Speaking of HDR, the Plus Series supports both Dolby Vision and HDR10+ .

Additionally, as it comes from Roku itself, the Plus Series offers a seamless implementation of the Roku experience. The interface is smooth and easy to use. You also have private listening support and more. Although the TV lacks advanced gaming features, such as a 120Hz panel and VRR, Roku has ensured gamers will get low input lag, along with Auto Low Latency Mode (ALLM) support.

You can buy the Roku Plus Series in 55-inch , 65-inch , and 75-inch sizes.

Best Roku TV Overall

Roku Plus Series

Enjoy impressive 4K picture, Dolby Vision HDR, and Roku’s easy-to-use software in the company’s Plus Series TV. It’s also a good option for casual gaming.

Best Roku TV Under $500: Roku Select Series

Roku

Pros Cons
HDR10+ support Lacks advanced gaming features
Low input lag and ALLM No local dimming or Dolby Vision HDR
Multiple sizes under $500

Don’t have the budget for the Roku Plus Series? Don’t worry. The company offers a step-down model in the form of the Select Series , which is more affordable. Although it doesn’t have the fancier technologies such as quantum dots or full-array local dimming, the TV produces a decent picture. It won’t be able to compete with more expensive TVs, but for its price tag, the visual performance of the Select Series won’t disappoint you.

The Roku Select Series TV is compatible with HDR and can display HDR10+ and HDR10 signals without any trouble. Plus, you get four HDMI ports, including one that supports eARC .

In other highlights, casual gamers will also be happy with the Select Series as it has a low input lag for a responsive gaming experience and can automatically switch to the Game Mode thanks to ALLM support. But as you can expect, Roku had to make a few sacrifices to reach the lower price tag of the Select Series. The TV not only lacks the advanced gaming features like the Plus Series, but it also doesn’t have Dolby Vision support.

You can buy the 4K Roku Select Series TV in a variety of sizes from 43 inches to 75 inches , with only the biggest size going beyond the $500 threshold.

Best Roku TV Under $500

Roku Select TV

The Select Series is Roku’s affordable version of the Plus Series. It has 4K resolution, HDR10+ support, and a low input lag for gaming.

Best Roku TV Under $300: Hisense 43R6E3

Hisense

Pros Cons
Good native contrast ratio and deep blacks Low HDR brightness
Dolby Vision and HDR10 support Average performance
Excellent value for money

The Hisense 43R6E3 is one of the most affordable 4K Roku TVs on the market. If you’re looking for an inexpensive Roku television for a guest room or a dorm room, it’s your best bet.

The television looks decent and has narrow bezels, but apart from that, it’s mostly a practical design. Thanks to the TV’s VA panel, its native contrast ratio is good, and the company has also included Dolby Vision and HDR10 support. However, the 43R6E3 doesn’t get bright enough to use the HDR support effectively.

Still, the 43R6E3 offers a decent viewing experience for its price tag, and you won’t be disappointed. As expected, there are no advanced gaming features, but Hisense has included a game mode that offers relatively low input lag and okay response time.

If you can spend more money, the TCL 43S435 is another excellent budget Roku TV. It has an MSRP of $349, but you can get it for as low as $290. The TV offers an excellent contrast ratio and can display deep blacks, which is an upgrade from Hisense’s set. If you can wait until the TCL model is on sale, you won’t regret it.

Best Roku TV Under $300

Hisense 43R6E3

The Hisense 43R6E3 is a good option for anyone looking for an inexpensive Roku TV. It costs less than $300 and provides a decent viewing experience.

Best Roku TV For Gaming: Hisense U6GR

Hisense

Pros Cons
VRR support and low input lag Trouble displaying 480p signals
Dolby Vision and HDR10+ support No 120Hz panel or HDMI 2.1 ports
Good picture quality

Although a slightly older TV, the Hisense U6GR is a great 4K model that comes with baked-in Roku OS. And thanks to its low input lag, support for VRR, and ALLM, it’s a good option for gaming.

The TV also shines on the picture quality front as Hisense uses a VA-type LCD panel with full-array local dimming. So the TV’s already good black levels get enhanced, and you get an excellent contrast ratio. The vibrant and lifelike colors from the U6GR’s quantum dot layer pop on it, making watching movies and playing games a treat.

Other features include support for Dolby Vision and HDR10+, giving you access to most HDR content. Moreover, the U6GR is good at upscaling 720p or 1080p content but struggles with 480p . Additionally, as the Hisense U6GR is missing a 120Hz panel, it’s not a suitable television for anyone wanting to enjoy high-refresh-rate gaming. But as the only Roku TV with a 120Hz panel—the TCL 6 Series R655 —is pretty hard to find, your best alternative will be to pick one of the best gaming TV picks and use a Roku streaming stick to get the Roku experience.

Otherwise, the Hisense U6GR is an all-around good gaming Roku TV with a few missing features. You can buy it in 55-inch and 65-inch models.

Best Roku TV for Gaming

Hisense U6GR QLED Smart TV

The budget-friendly Hisense U6GR is a good option for gaming, thanks to its low input lag, VRR support, and a 4K panel. It also offers a fantastic picture quality.

Best Roku TV For Movies: Roku Plus Series

Roku

Pros Cons
Excellent picture quality Unable to remove judder
Above-average audio performance Narrow viewing angles
Dolby Vision and HDR10+ support

After years of working with TV manufacturers like Hisense and TCL to release Roku TVs, the company introduced its first self-branded TVs in 2023 . And its Plus Series televisions are pretty great for a first attempt. Sitting at the top of Roku’s TV lineup, the Plus Series packs nearly all the bells and whistles you need for a fantastic movie-watching experience.

It has a 4K LCD panel with quantum dot technology and full-array local dimming. As a result, you get a vibrant picture and deep blacks. HDR is also available, supporting Dolby Vision, HDR10+, and HDR10. Plus, the TV gets bright enough for a decent HDR experience.

Another highlight of the Roku Plus Series is its sound quality. Unlike most modern televisions that pack average speakers and often need a soundbar or speaker system for excellent audio, the Roku TV offers a decent audio experience with rich sound.

Other features include four HDMI ports, including one for eARC. Dolby Atmos support is also present, and the Plus Series has decent upscaling for lower-resolution content.

Sadly, the Roku Plus Series is not without flaws. One significant issue with the Roku Plus Series is its inability to remove judder . So if you are sensitive to judder , it’s might be best to go for the TCL 6 Series R655 . The TCL TV is more expensive, but you get better picture quality and gaming performance. However, the TCL model has trouble upscaling 480p content.

Best Roku TV For Movies

Roku Plus Series

Roku’s flagship Plus Series shines in picture quality, audio performance, and HDR experience. It’s also pretty good at upscaling.
